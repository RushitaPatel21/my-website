Evaluating LLMs using Construction Scenario Prompts

Brief: This project analyzes how different prompting styles, context levels, and role-based instructions influence the accuracy and quality of AI-generated responses for real construction situations.

The focus is on practical tasks like RFIs, change orders, safety notes, and coordination messages—evaluating how well various LLMs handle risk, sequencing, technical detail, and clear next steps.

Scenario example: Draft a Change Order Request (COR) for relocating tray and 4-inch EMT at Area A-110, including justification, schedule impacts for night work, and coordination with other trades.

Creation of GPT – Construction Scenario Assistant

Brief: A custom GPT assistant designed specifically for construction field workflows—helping teams draft RFIs, CORs, safety notes, and coordination messages using structured prompts and project-specific context.

The experiment focuses on prompt templates, guardrails, and reference documents that keep responses aligned with construction standards and realistic site conditions.

Scenario example: Ask the assistant to prepare a coordination email to an electrical subcontractor summarizing conflicts found in the ceiling space and requesting updated layouts before the next coordination meeting.

AI Tools in Construction

Brief: A survey of AI-enabled tools currently used across the construction lifecycle—covering document control, schedule analysis, progress tracking, safety, and model-based coordination.

The deliverable compares capabilities, limitations, and use cases, with a focus on how each tool fits into real project workflows for field engineers and project managers.

Scenario example: Evaluate which AI tools would best support a mission-critical data center job that requires heavy coordination, fast issue closure, and detailed progress documentation.

Final Project – AI-Based Construction Delay Predictor

Brief: Graduate applied project focused on developing an AI-based delay prediction model for construction activities, using project data such as durations, labor, weather, and change events.

The work includes dataset design, feature engineering, model selection, and dashboard concepts that help project teams identify early-warning signals for potential delays.

Scenario example: Predict the probability of delay for a concrete pour activity based on historical productivity, crew size, weather records, and recent change orders affecting the work area.
